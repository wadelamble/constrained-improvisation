## Proposed High-Level Concept List

1. Dynamics begins with histories.
Mechanics is constrained optimization over possible histories, not first a force law.

2. Action is spacetime-native in the free case.
For a free relativistic particle, the action is built solely from spacetime interval/worldline length, up to scale.

3. Straightness is geometry-relative.
A physical path is "straightest" only relative to the geometry or field structure used to compare nearby states/events.

4. Curvature is loop mismatch.
Curvature can be introduced intrinsically as the failure of two small moves around a loop to agree; later this becomes commutator/field strength.

5. Free motion is geodesic motion.
A free particle does not feel force; it follows the straightest available worldline.

6. Felt force means projection failure.
A particle may be straight in a larger/full geometric structure while appearing accelerated in spacetime.

7. Potentials are effective summaries.
`T + V` mechanics works when the field shaping motion is treated as fixed, external, or nondynamical.

8. Potential gradients demand ontology.
If a function on spacetime changes motion, a fundamental theory should explain its physical source, dynamics, and coupling.

9. Functions on spaces have interpretive choices.
A function may be a scoring rule, a potential, a frozen field, a metric factor, a connection component, or a dynamical physical field.

10. Geometry can absorb force.
Jacobi/Eisenhart-style moves show that potentials can be rephrased as geometry, though often by fixing energy or enlarging the arena.

11. GR makes metric geometry physical.
Gravity is the case where the relevant curvature is spacetime geometry itself, dynamically coupled to stress-energy.

12. Gauge makes comparison rules physical.
Gauge theory promotes local comparison conventions into connection fields whose curvature produces force.

13. The finance analogy teaches gauge without QM.
Currency/exchange-rate loops give an intuitive model of local convention, connection, curvature, and induced flow.

14. Local symmetry requires a connection.
If internal labels can vary independently point by point, derivatives must be corrected by a connection to preserve invariance.

15. Gauge curvature is a commutator.
The field strength is the failure of covariant moves to commute: `[D_mu, D_nu]`.

16. Fields must contribute to action.
Physicality means the field has its own action term, degrees of freedom, and possible waves.

17. Matter and geometry co-determine dynamics.
Matter/current shapes field curvature; field curvature redirects matter/current.

18. Toy gauge is not yet full QFT.
The complex scalar model should be framed as a classical/toy gauge field model, not as "the real quantum theory."

19. LM hands off to Differential Mechanics.
Action variation produces boundary terms; boundary sensitivity reveals momentum and the local state variables.

20. Conservative dynamics preserves information.
Hamiltonian flow preserves phase-space measure, turning mechanics into a theory of information-preserving state flow.
