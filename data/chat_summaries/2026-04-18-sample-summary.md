# Sample Summary: Technical Writing Preferences

- date: 2026-04-18
- source: Sample seed note

## Project

Build a writing agent that can accumulate durable guidance from long conversations about technical writing.

## Audience

- Technical readers who want clear explanations without unnecessary jargon.
- Mixed-skill teams where some readers are experts and others are still onboarding.

## Principles

- Prefer concrete examples before abstractions.
- Keep tone warm, direct, and unsnarky.
- Avoid bloated intros and repetitive hedging.
- Preserve nuance without burying the main point.

## Structural Decisions

- Maintain a living outline instead of treating each draft as isolated.
- Separate auto-generated guidance from manual editorial judgment.
- Store distilled summaries instead of raw chat dumps as the primary knowledge base.

## Reusable Phrasing

- Start broad, then move specific.
- Explain the tradeoff, then recommend the default.
- Make the next step obvious.

## Open Questions

- How much authority should the agent have to reorder sections?
- Should conflicting advice be merged, ranked, or flagged for review?
