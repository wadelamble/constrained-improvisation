## The Symplectic Geometry of Phase Space and Hamiltonian Dynamics: Claim Map

### Movement 1: From histories to states

Purpose: Explain how a theory defined on whole paths yields structure belonging to a state at one time.

- The action is a functional of an entire history.
- Varying the action separates into a bulk term and a boundary term.
- The bulk term determines the physical path.
- The boundary term records how the action changes when an endpoint state is changed.
- Once the question becomes "what counts as a state at one time?", the boundary term becomes structurally decisive.

### Movement 2: Why momentum appears

Purpose: Earn \(p_i = \partial L/\partial \dot q^i\) as the quantity paired with displacement.

- The boundary variation has the form \(p_i\,\delta q^i\).
- This makes \(p_i\) the coefficient of endpoint displacement.
- Velocity is path-rate data, not yet state-pairing data.
- Canonical momentum is selected by the variational structure itself.
- The exact form of momentum depends on the Lagrangian, and in free systems the kinetic part of that Lagrangian is rooted in spacetime kinematics.

### Movement 3: From the one-form to the two-form

Purpose: Show why the pairing first appears as a one-form but Liouville forces attention to a two-form.

- The pairing \(p_i\,\delta q^i\) is a one-form measuring first-order action-response to one infinitesimal state change.
- Liouville is about clouds of nearby states, not one infinitesimal direction.
- One direction gives only line data; a generic ensemble in a two-dimensional phase space requires area data.
- The first local object that measures a patch of nearby states is \(dq^i \wedge dp_i\).
- The two-form is therefore the first genuinely ensemble-facing local object.

### Movement 4: Why the two-form is intrinsic

Purpose: Show that the two-form is not just larger than the one-form, but more invariant.

- The one-form depends on the choice of Lagrangian representative.
- Adding a total time derivative can change the one-form without changing the equations of motion.
- The two-form built from the one-form is insensitive to that ambiguity.
- The logic chain is: action \(\rightarrow\) pairing \(\rightarrow\) one-form \(\rightarrow\) intrinsic two-form \(\rightarrow\) local patches of state space.

### Movement 5: What phase space is

Purpose: Define phase space only after the conjugate pairing has been earned.

- Phase space is the space whose points are instantaneous states expressed in the conjugate variables selected by the action.
- For one degree of freedom a point is \((q,p)\); for many degrees of freedom it is \((q^1,\dots,q^n,p_1,\dots,p_n)\).
- Phase space is not, in general, position-velocity space.
- The move to phase space is a reorganization of what counts as the instantaneous state.

### Movement 6: Why the Legendre transform belongs here

Purpose: Explain the Hamiltonian rewrite as a consequence of the already-earned pairing.

- Once \(p_i = \partial L/\partial \dot q^i\) has been selected, the next problem is to rewrite the theory in terms of \((q,p)\) rather than \((q,\dot q)\).
- A Legendre transform may transform only a chosen subset of variables, leaving the others as parameters.
- In mechanics the transform is taken with respect to the velocity variables \(\dot q^i\), not the position variables \(q^i\).
- The Legendre transform does not create the pairing; it follows the pairing already exposed by the action.
- Its mechanical significance is the cancellation of the unwanted \(d\dot q^i\) terms in \(dH\).
- After the Euler-Lagrange equations are used, the differential of \(H\) takes the cross-coupled form from which Hamilton's equations can be read off.

### Movement 7: Turning the movie on

Purpose: End the section by making the earned geometry visible in motion without yet importing the full generator or Poisson-bracket story.

- A point of phase space is one exact instantaneous state.
- As time evolves, that point traces a curve.
- A family of nearby points traces a moving patch.
- That patch is the first visible object on which the Liouville story can later be seen.
- The fluid analogy may color this ending, but phase-space flow must do the explanatory work.
- Generator language, contour-map language, and bracket language belong later or only anticipatorily.
