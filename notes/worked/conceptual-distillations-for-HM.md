# Conceptual Distillations for HM

Working note from 2026-05-14.

Functions are identities in Hamiltonian Mechanics. State is not.

State is a few numbers that point the identity in its next direction. In QM, by contrast, just as in HM we put functions in front of vector fields, in QM we put vectors in front of functions.

Each move introduces a new constraint.

In HM, the whole trick is to be able to integrate: to convert a couple of numbers into a history. But to do that, what do you integrate? A function. That is already a constraint. Observables must be functions so that they are integrable.

In QM, the corresponding move is stricter. To preserve not just the form of functions, but also the overlap between functions, a vector is introduced. This is the new constraint that follows from states as superpositions.

Possible bridge formulation:

- HM: functions carry identity; states supply enough numbers to aim the identity into a history.
- HM constraint: observables must be functions because the theory must integrate them into flows/histories.
- QM: vectors carry superposed state; functions/operators must preserve not only form but overlap.
- QM constraint: the state space must support superposition and inner-product preservation.

The conceptual bridge is not "QM is HM with different variables." It is that HM already teaches the discipline of asking what must be preserved when local data is integrated into lawful evolution. QM tightens the preservation condition: not only the shape of observables, but the overlap structure among possible states.
